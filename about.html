On the subject
of Aamina

Some people fill a room. Aamina fills the quiet moments too.

There's a particular kind of warmth that doesn't announce itself — it just settles into a room, into a conversation, into an ordinary Tuesday, and makes it worth remembering. That's the closest I can get to describing her.

She has a way of listening that makes you feel like the only person in the world worth hearing. A laugh that arrives suddenly and completely, like it's been waiting for permission. A kind of patience with the world — and with the people in it — that seems, some days, almost unfair to the rest of us.

This page won't do her justice. No page could. But it's here anyway, because some things are worth saying even imperfectly: that she is thought of often, appreciated more than she probably realizes, and loved — plainly, steadily, without conditions attached.

If you're reading this, Aamina — this whole small corner of the internet was built with exactly one person in mind. It's you. It was always going to be you.
